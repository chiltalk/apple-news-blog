Best Apple Trade-In Values in 2026: Is It Worth Trading In Old Devices?

March 28, 2026 • Apple News Daily Staff • 9 min read

Apple's Trade-In program offers convenient device recycling with store credit or gift cards, but how do values compare to third-party alternatives? We analyzed current trade-in rates for iPhones, iPads, Macs, and Apple Watches to determine whether Apple's program or competing services provide better returns for your old devices.

iPhone Trade-In Values: Where Apple Leads

iPhone values remain strongest in the used market, and Apple's program reflects this reality. iPhone 15 Pro Max in good condition trades for $480-520 through Apple, while iPhone 14 Pro maxes out at $350-380. The newer the generation and larger the storage, the more value retains. iPhone 13 models still command $200-250, a remarkable retention rate for four-year-old technology.

Apple's valuations update frequently based on market conditions, and the current rates represent a slight increase from 2025 as demand for budget-friendly iPhones increases. The company competes aggressively with Gazelle, Decluttr, and carrier trade-in programs, often providing better values for devices in excellent condition with original accessories and packaging.

However, Apple's condition requirements remain strict. Screen scratches, dented corners, or battery health below 80% triggers reduced valuations or program disqualification. Users with pristine devices receive top dollar, while those with visible wear often fare better selling directly through eBay or Facebook Marketplace where cosmetic issues affect prices less dramatically.

iPad Trade-In: Solid Returns for Tablets

iPad values have stabilized after years of decline as tablet replacement cycles lengthen. iPad Pro M4 models retain $500-650 depending on condition and storage, while iPad Air M2 versions trade for $300-400. The base iPad 10th generation still commands $200-250, demonstrating that even budget iPads maintain meaningful resale value.

Apple's iPad trade-in values compare favorably to third-party services, though Swappa and eBay occasionally yield higher returns for sellers willing to list individually and ship internationally. The convenience premium—shipping prepaid boxes, immediate verification, fast payment—costs approximately 10-15% versus private sales for most iPad models.

iPad Mini values have surprised analysts, with strong demand from students and travelers keeping 6th generation models above $200. The compact form factor fills a niche that larger tablets cannot, and Apple's trade-in program reflects this sustained demand with competitive valuations.

Mac Trade-In: Strongest Value Retention

Macs represent Apple's strongest trade-in category, with M-series chips delivering unprecedented longevity. MacBook Pro M3 models retain 60-70% of original MSRP depending on configuration, while M2 MacBook Airs hold 50-55% values. The performance advantages of Apple Silicon translate directly to resale premiums that Intel Macs cannot match.

Desktop Macs show even stronger retention. Mac Studio M4 Ultra configurations valued at $3,000+ trade for $2,200-2,500, representing remarkable residual values for professional workstations. Mac Mini M4 delivers the strongest percentage retention, with $600 models selling for $400-450 on the used market.

Apple's Mac trade-in values fall slightly behind private sale prices but dramatically exceed carrier and big-box retailer programs. For businesses with multiple Macs to refresh, Apple's Business Device Trade-In program provides volume assessment and enterprise billing that simplifies equipment cycles. The streamlined process often justifies the 5-10% discount versus maximizing every dollar through individual sales.

Apple Watch Trade-In: Decent but Variable

Apple Watch values fluctuate significantly based on generation and cellular capability. Apple Watch Ultra 3 trades for $400-450, while Ultra 2 drops to $300-320. Series 9 models range $150-200 depending on size and cellular, with SE versions falling below $120. The rapid generational changes in health sensors make older Apple Watches depreciate faster than iPhones.

GPS-only versus cellular capability creates meaningful value differences. A Series 9 cellular model commands $50-70 premium over GPS-only versions in similar condition. For buyers seeking connectivity without iPhone proximity, cellular capability justifies the premium and maintains residual values more effectively.

Trade-in values for Apple Watch show the most variance between Apple's program and third-party alternatives. Gazelle and Decluttr often offer 15-20% less than Apple for modern models, while specialized wearable resellers occasionally pay more for devices with pristine screens and original bands.

When Trade-In Beats Selling Directly

Apple's Trade-In program provides advantages beyond simple valuation convenience. The process requires no photography, listing, buyer negotiation, or shipping coordination. Devices are typically evaluated within three business days of receipt, and payment arrives as store credit immediately upon approval. For busy professionals, the time savings often exceed the value difference.

Safety concerns favor trade-in programs. Meeting strangers from Facebook Marketplace or shipping valuable electronics through carriers involves risks that institutional programs eliminate. Apple's program provides tracking, insurance, and guaranteed payment that private sales cannot match.

When trading toward a new Apple device, the process becomes particularly seamless. Store credit applies directly to new purchases, and AppleCare+ purchases can be combined with trade-in credits for single-transaction upgrades. The integration removes friction that might otherwise delay device refreshes.

When to Sell Instead of Trade-In

Devices with cosmetic damage or functional issues often sell better privately than through trade-in programs. Apple's strict condition requirements reduce valuations for scratched screens, dented corners, or battery degradation. Private buyers frequently accept imperfect devices at prices that exceed trade-in payouts for the same hardware.

Rare configurations command premiums in private markets. iPhones with unusual storage capacities, limited edition colors, or original packaging intact sell for 10-20% above trade-in values to collectors who value these attributes. Apple's program pays based on model and condition without recognizing these variables.

International markets sometimes offer superior prices for devices with strong demand. Southeast Asian markets, Eastern European countries, and emerging economies show consistent demand for iPhones at price points below new devices. Export through dedicated services captures these geographic arbitrage opportunities that domestic trade-in programs cannot reflect.

Third-Party Alternatives Worth Considering

Gazelle remains the most established third-party option, offering competitive valuations with a 30-day price lock guarantee. The company has refined its inspection process over fifteen years, and payouts through PayPal or check arrive within five business days of device verification. Gazelle often matches or beats Apple for specific device categories.

Decluttr provides the fastest payment among major services, with same-day payment upon verification for most devices. The company's valuations run 5-10% below competitors but the certainty and speed appeal to sellers prioritizing cash flow over maximum return.

Carrier trade-in programs through AT&T, Verizon, and T-Mobile occasionally offer promotional bonuses that exceed Apple values. These programs require new device activation on installment plans, tying your trade-in to a carrier switch or upgrade. The restrictions make carrier programs most valuable when simultaneously changing service providers.

Maximizing Your Trade-In Value

Regardless of chosen program, device presentation affects final valuations. Original boxes, unused cables, and documentation add $10-30 to Apple trade-in values and significantly improve private sale prices. Keeping accessories matters more for value retention than most users realize.

Battery health represents the most impactful single factor for iPhone valuations. Replacing an aging battery for $69 through Apple before trading can increase valuations by $50-100, creating net positive returns for the investment. This improvement applies across all trade-in programs and private sales.

Factory resetting devices before trade-in prevents data retention concerns and sometimes triggers higher valuations. Apple's verification process checks for personal data before final approval, and clean devices process faster than those requiring remote wipe during evaluation.

The Verdict: Trade-In Convenience vs Maximum Return

Apple's Trade-In program delivers competitive valuations with unmatched convenience for most users. The 10-15% convenience premium—versus private sales—represents reasonable compensation for eliminated hassle, guaranteed payment, and simplified device cycling. Most users should feel confident using Apple's program as their default trade-in path.

However, power users with pristine devices, unusual configurations, or time available for listing and shipping can consistently outperform Apple's valuations. The gap typically ranges 10-25% depending on device category and condition, enough to justify effort for those trading multiple devices or owning rare configurations.

The used electronics market continues maturing, with trade-in programs competing more aggressively than ever for device volume. Apple's program remains strongest for iPhones and Macs, while third-party services occasionally outperform for specific device categories. Checking multiple valuations before committing ensures you capture the best available return for your specific device.
