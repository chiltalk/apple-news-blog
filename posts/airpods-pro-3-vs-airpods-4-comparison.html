AirPods Pro 3 vs AirPods 4: Which Should You Buy?

March 28, 2026 • Apple News Daily Staff • 8 min read

With Apple now offering two distinct AirPods Pro generations and the standard AirPods 4, choosing the right Apple earbuds has become more complicated. Here's our comprehensive breakdown to help you decide.

The Current Lineup

Apple's wireless earbuds lineup has evolved significantly. The AirPods Pro 3 represent the premium, most advanced option, while the AirPods 4 sit as the mid-range choice with an open-fit design. Both feature USB-C charging and Apple's latest H3 chip, but the differences in design, features, and price create distinct use cases.

The AirPods Pro 3 are priced at $249, while the AirPods 4 with Active Noise Cancellation cost $179. The standard AirPods 4 without ANC are available for $129, though we recommend the ANC model for most users given the significant feature premium.

Design and Comfort

The fundamental difference between these models is the ear tip design. AirPods Pro 3 use an in-ear design with silicone tips that create a seal within the ear canal. This provides passive noise isolation in addition to active noise cancellation. The trade-off is that some users find in-ear earbuds uncomfortable for extended wear.

AirPods 4 retain the open-fit design of previous standard AirPods, sitting in the outer ear rather than sealing the canal. Many users find this design more comfortable for all-day wear, and it eliminates the "plugged ear" sensation some people experience with in-ear designs. The AirPods 4 are notably smaller and lighter than the AirPods Pro 3.

AirPods Pro 3 come with four sizes of silicone tips (XS, S, M, L) to ensure a proper fit, while AirPods 4 rely on their universal shape. If you've struggled with AirPods falling out, the Pro 3's secure fit and tip options may be preferable. If you prefer the natural feel of open-ear buds, AirPods 4 are the clear choice.

Sound Quality

Both models deliver excellent audio quality, but there are meaningful differences. AirPods Pro 3 feature a new custom Apple driver and amplifier that produce richer bass and more detailed highs. The in-ear design creates a more intimate soundstage with better low-end response, as the sealed environment prevents bass leakage.

AirPods 4 have surprised reviewers with how good they sound for an open-fit design. The new driver and computational audio improvements deliver clean, balanced sound that exceeds expectations. However, audiophiles and bass enthusiasts will likely prefer the Pro 3's deeper, more impactful low-end.

Both models support Personalized Spatial Audio with dynamic head tracking, creating an immersive theater-like experience for compatible content. The Pro 3 may have a slight edge in Spatial Audio performance due to the more controlled acoustic environment.

Active Noise Cancellation and Transparency

This is where the AirPods Pro 3 pull significantly ahead. The Pro 3 feature Apple's most advanced ANC yet, with improved feedforward and feedback microphones that cancel approximately 2x more noise than the previous generation. In testing, Pro 3 ANC rivals dedicated over-ear headphones for consistent low-frequency noise reduction.

The AirPods 4 with ANC offer respectable noise cancellation for an open-fit design, but physics limitations prevent them from matching the Pro 3. They handle sudden noises and moderate ambient sound well, but struggle with deep bass frequencies that the Pro 3 eliminate effortlessly.

Transparency mode is excellent on both models, with the Pro 3's Adaptive Transparency being particularly impressive. It automatically attenuates harsh sounds (construction, sirens) while letting ambient noise through naturally. AirPods 4 also feature transparency mode, though without the adaptive capabilities.

Battery Life

Battery life is competitive between the two models when accounting for the charging case. AirPods Pro 3 deliver up to 6 hours of listening time (ANC on), extended to 30 hours with the charging case. AirPods 4 provide up to 5 hours (ANC on), with the case extending to 25 hours total.

Both cases support wireless charging (MagSafe, Apple Watch charger, Qi), and USB-C charging. The Pro 3 case includes a built-in speaker for Find My alerts, while the AirPods 4 case omits this for the standard model.

Features Comparison

Beyond the core differences, several features distinguish these models:

  • Hearing Health: Pro 3 include a built-in hearing aid capability and hearing test feature, making them the choice for users with mild hearing loss
  • Conversation Awareness: Both models lower volume automatically when you start speaking, but Pro 3 respond more precisely
  • Touch Controls: Pro 3 offer swipe-based volume control on the stem; AirPods 4 use tap controls
  • Water Resistance: Pro 3 are IP54 rated (dust and splash resistant); AirPods 4 are IP54 rated as well
  • Fit Testing: Pro 3 include an ear tip fit test in iOS; AirPods 4 do not

Which Should You Buy?

Choose AirPods Pro 3 if:

You prioritize the best possible noise cancellation for commutes, flights, or noisy environments. You want the richest possible sound quality. You prefer the secure, isolated fit of in-ear earbuds. You have mild hearing loss and want the built-in hearing aid functionality. You frequently make phone calls in noisy settings.

Choose AirPods 4 if:

You find in-ear earbuds uncomfortable and prefer the open-fit design. You primarily use earbuds at home, in the office, or in quiet environments. You value all-day comfort over maximum noise cancellation. You want the best balance of features and value. You're buying as a gift for someone new to AirPods.

Our Recommendation

For most users, the AirPods 4 with ANC at $179 represent the sweet spot of Apple's earbuds lineup. They deliver 85% of the Pro 3 experience at 70% of the price, with the added benefit of superior comfort for extended wear.

However, if you regularly commute, travel, or work in noisy environments, the AirPods Pro 3's superior ANC justifies the $70 premium. The hearing health features are also becoming increasingly relevant as Apple positions these devices as medical-grade consumer electronics.

Whatever you choose, both represent significant upgrades over previous AirPods generations. If you're coming from original AirPods, AirPods 2, or AirPods 3, either model will feel like a transformative improvement.
