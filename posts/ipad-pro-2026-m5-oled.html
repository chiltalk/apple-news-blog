iPad Pro 2026: M5 chip, OLED display, and what it means for professionals

March 28, 2026 • Apple News Daily Staff • 9 min read

Apple has unveiled the iPad Pro 2026, a dramatic overhaul of its professional tablet lineup that introduces the M5 chip and a stunning OLED display for the first time. The combination represents the most significant leap for the iPad Pro since the transition to Apple Silicon, and raises the bar for what a tablet can accomplish.

The M5 Chip: Desktop Power, Tablet Form Factor

At the core of the new iPad Pro sits Apple's M5 chip, built on TSMC's enhanced 3nm process. The chip features a 10-core CPU with four performance cores and six efficiency cores, delivering approximately 30% faster CPU performance compared to the M4. More impressively, the 10-core GPU offers up to 40% better graphics performance, with hardware-accelerated ray tracing and mesh shading that brings console-quality rendering to a tablet.

The Neural Engine has received substantial upgrades, now capable of 45 trillion operations per second. This enables on-device AI features that would have required cloud computing just a generation ago. Apple Intelligence, Apple's suite of AI tools, runs entirely on-device for most tasks, preserving user privacy while delivering responsive performance.

Unified memory options range from 16GB to 64GB, with the higher-end configurations designed specifically for professionals running multiple professional applications simultaneously. Bandwidth has increased to 400GB/s, ensuring the chip never waits for data.

OLED Display: A Visual Revolution

The most visually striking change is the introduction of OLED display technology to the iPad Pro lineup. After years of using Liquid Retina LCD panels, Apple has made the switch to OLED, delivering true blacks, infinite contrast ratios, and colors that truly pop.

The 11-inch model features a 2420 x 1668 resolution display, while the 13-inch model offers 2752 x 2064 pixels. Both support ProMotion with adaptive refresh rates up to 120Hz, and both achieve peak brightness of 1600 nits for HDR content. The typical full-screen brightness sits at 1000 nits, making the new iPad Pro the brightest tablet Apple has ever produced.

For creative professionals, the OLED display supports the P3 wide color gamut and includes a new "Reference Mode" that ensures color accuracy critical for video and photo editing work. This makes the iPad Pro 2026 a viable reference monitor for color grading in the field.

The display also introduces "Ultra Retina OLED," Apple's branding for a new tandem OLED architecture that stacks two OLED panels. This innovation allows for higher brightness levels than standard OLED while maintaining the deep blacks and energy efficiency the technology is known for.

Professional Workflows Transformed

The combination of M5 performance and OLED display has meaningful implications for professional workflows. Video editors working in DaVinci Resolve or Adobe Premiere Pro will appreciate the improved color accuracy and the faster render times enabled by the M5's media engine, which includes dedicated hardware for ProRes and HEVC encoding and decoding.

3D artists using apps like Shapr3D or Octane Render will benefit from the faster GPU and improved ray tracing capabilities. The OLED display's true blacks provide a more accurate representation of how content will appear in production, whether for digital advertising or film work.

Architects and CAD professionals have long relied on iPad Pro for site visits and client presentations. The new model offers enough performance to run complex models directly on the device, with the OLED display presenting designs in their most accurate colors.

Music producers using Logic Pro for iPad will find that the M5 can handle more tracks, more plugins, and more real-time processing than ever before. The improved thermal performance ensures the iPad Pro can maintain peak performance for extended sessions without throttling.

Battery Life and Connectivity

Despite the more power-hungry OLED display, Apple claims battery life remains competitive at up to 10 hours of web browsing or video playback. The efficiency cores in the M5 chip work in concert with OLED's per-pixel lighting to manage power consumption intelligently.

Connectivity includes Thunderbolt 4 with USB-C, supporting external drives, displays up to 6K resolution, and eGPUs for additional graphics power when needed. Wi-Fi 7 and Bluetooth 5.3 provide the latest wireless standards, and 5G variants remain available for mobile connectivity.

Apple Pencil and Magic Keyboard

The new iPad Pro supports the latest Apple Pencil Pro, which includes a new haptic feedback engine and a gyroscope for precise tilt and rotation detection. The hover feature from previous models continues, showing a preview of brush strokes or selections before they make contact with the screen.

The updated Magic Keyboard attaches magnetically and includes a new row of function keys, a larger trackpad with haptic feedback, and an aluminum palm rest that brings the typing experience closer to a traditional laptop. The keyboard still folds into a protective case but now offers two viewing angles thanks to a redesigned hinge mechanism.

Pricing and Availability

The iPad Pro 2026 starts at $999 for the 11-inch Wi-Fi model with 256GB of storage and 16GB of unified memory. The 13-inch model begins at $1,199. The cellular variants add $200 to each. Configure-to-order options include 32GB memory ($100), 48GB memory ($300), 64GB memory ($500), 512GB storage ($100), 1TB storage ($300), and 2TB storage ($500).

For comparison, a fully configured 13-inch iPad Pro with 64GB memory and 2TB storage reaches $2,699. The new Apple Pencil Pro costs $129, and the updated Magic Keyboard is available for $299.

Early Impressions

The iPad Pro 2026 represents Apple's clearest statement yet that the iPad Pro is a serious computer for serious professionals. The combination of M5 power, OLED visuals, and an ecosystem of professional applications continues to narrow the gap between tablet and traditional computing.

For current iPad Pro M4 owners, the decision to upgrade depends heavily on workflow. Creative professionals who demand the best display technology and maximum performance will find compelling reasons to upgrade. For everyone else, the M4 remains an excellent machine, and the value proposition of the M4 will only improve as it ages.

The OLED display alone justifies attention. Whether you're editing photos, watching HDR video, or presenting to clients, the visual improvement is immediately noticeable. Combined with the substantial performance gains of the M5, the iPad Pro 2026 is not merely an incremental improvement but a generational leap that reaffirms Apple's commitment to the professional tablet market.
