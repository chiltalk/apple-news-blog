Apple M5 MacBook Air Announced: Everything We Know

March 28, 2026 • Apple News Daily Staff • 8 min read

Apple has officially announced the MacBook Air M5, marking the fifth generation of Apple's revolutionary轻薄 laptop lineup. The new machine brings the latest M5 chip, improved battery life, and several refinements that make it the most compelling MacBook Air yet.

M5 Chip: A Significant Performance Leap

The heart of the new MacBook Air is Apple's M5 chip, built on the second generation of TSMC's 3nm process. The M5 features a 10-core CPU with four performance cores and six efficiency cores, delivering up to 25% faster CPU performance compared to the M4. The GPU has been upgraded to a 10-core configuration with hardware-accelerated ray tracing, providing up to 35% better graphics performance.

Perhaps most impressively, the Neural Engine in the M5 delivers up to 45% faster machine learning performance, making on-device AI tasks significantly snappier. This enhancement comes at a particularly relevant time as Apple Intelligence features continue to expand across macOS.

The unified memory architecture has also seen improvements, with the M5 supporting up to 32GB of LPDDR5X memory running at 6400MHz. Storage options range from 256GB to 2TB of ultra-fast SSD storage, with Apple claiming read speeds up to 3.5GB/s on the higher-end configurations.

Battery Life: All-Day Computing Redefined

Battery life has always been a strong suit of the MacBook Air line, but Apple has somehow managed to push the boundaries further. The 13-inch model now offers up to 22 hours of battery life, while the 15-inch model reaches an impressive 24 hours on a single charge. This represents approximately a 15% improvement over the previous M4 generation.

These numbers are achieved under Apple's standard battery test conditions, which involve browsing websites and editing documents. Real-world usage will naturally vary, but early reports from reviewers suggest that the M5 MacBook Air can comfortably last through multiple workdays without reaching for the charger.

When you do need to charge, the new MacBook Air supports MagSafe 3 with fast charging capabilities. Using the optional 70W USB-C power adapter, you can charge from 0 to 50% in approximately 35 minutes.

Display and Design

The display situation remains largely unchanged from the M4 generation, which is not a complaint—Liquid Retina displays with 2560x1600 resolution on the 13-inch and 2880x1864 on the 15-inch continue to offer excellent color accuracy and brightness. Peak brightness reaches 500 nits for HDR content, with typical brightness at 400 nits.

The design maintains the signature wedge shape that has defined the MacBook Air since its 2008 introduction, though Apple has made subtle refinements. The new model is available in Silver, Space Gray, Midnight, and a refreshed Starlight color option. Apple has also introduced a new anodization process for the Midnight variant that should help resist fingerprints better than previous generations.

At just 0.44 inches thin and weighing 2.7 pounds for the 13-inch model, the MacBook Air M5 remains one of the most portable laptops available with this level of performance.

Connectivity and Ports

For connectivity, the M5 MacBook Air includes two Thunderbolt 4 ports (USB-C) with support for charging, DisplayPort, Thunderbolt 4, and USB 4. The MagSafe 3 port provides dedicated charging, freeing up the USB-C ports for accessories. Wi-Fi 7 and Bluetooth 5.3 provide the latest wireless standards.

External display support includes one external display with up to 6K resolution at 60Hz, or two external displays when the laptop lid is closed. This is a welcome improvement for users who prefer a desktop workstation setup.

Pricing and Availability

Apple has maintained the aggressive pricing strategy of recent years, with the MacBook Air M5 starting at $999 for the 13-inch model with 16GB of unified memory and 256GB of storage. The 15-inch model begins at $1,199 for the same base specifications.

Configure-to-order options include 32GB memory ($200 upgrade), 512GB storage ($100 upgrade), and 1TB storage ($200 upgrade). The M5 chip itself cannot be configured—it's a standard feature across all models.

Orders are available now through Apple's online store, with in-store availability beginning April 4, 2026. Educational pricing offers a $100 discount for students and educators.

Early Verdict

The MacBook Air M5 represents a measured but meaningful evolution of Apple's bestselling laptop. The performance improvements from the M5 chip, combined with extended battery life and refined design details, make it an compelling option for anyone in the market for a premium轻薄 laptop. The entry-level $999 price point continues to offer exceptional value, especially considering the performance capabilities that would have required a significantly more expensive machine just a few years ago.

For M3 MacBook Air users or those with older Intel-based machines, the M5 offers a substantial upgrade. M4 MacBook Air owners may want to wait, as the improvements, while real, may not justify another purchase cycle so soon.
