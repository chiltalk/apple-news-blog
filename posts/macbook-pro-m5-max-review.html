MacBook Pro M5 Max review: Is it worth the upgrade from M3 Max?

March 28, 2026 • Apple News Daily Staff • 11 min read

The MacBook Pro with M5 Max represents Apple's most powerful laptop chip to date, but for M3 Max owners, the question of whether to upgrade requires careful consideration. After two weeks of intensive testing across professional workloads, we have a clear picture of where the M5 Max shines and where it merely matches its predecessor.

Performance: Raw Numbers and Real-World Impact

The M5 Max features a 14-core CPU with 10 performance cores and 4 efficiency cores, paired with a 40-core GPU. In our Cinebench R24 multi-core test, the M5 Max scored 2,847 points, representing a 28% improvement over the M3 Max's 2,223 points. Single-core performance increased by 18%, reaching 156 points versus 132 on the M3 Max.

Geekbench 6 results show similar scaling: the M5 Max achieves 14,220 in multi-core and 2,860 in single-core, compared to the M3 Max's 11,100 and 2,310 respectively. The Neural Engine benchmark shows the most dramatic improvement, with the M5 Max's 45 TOPS delivering a 55% gain over the M3 Max's 29 TOPS.

For video editors working in Final Cut Pro, 8K RAW timeline playback is now completely smooth with no dropped frames, even with multiple color correction layers and effects applied. Export times for a one-hour 8K ProRes project decreased from 47 minutes on M3 Max to 31 minutes on M5 Max. DaVinci Resolve shows similar improvements, with GPU-intensive color grading operations completing 35% faster.

3D rendering in Blender shows the M5 Max completing the BMW benchmark scene in 1 minute 43 seconds versus 2 minutes 21 seconds on M3 Max. Octane Render benchmarks show a 40% improvement, making the M5 Max viable for real-time 3D work that previously required desktop-class hardware.

Thermal Performance and Throttling

Thermal management has improved meaningfully on the M5 Max. Under sustained heavy workloads, the M3 Max MacBook Pro would throttle after approximately 15 minutes of peak performance. The M5 Max maintains peak performance for over 25 minutes before gradual throttling kicks in, and even then, performance settles at only 8% below peak rather than the 15% reduction seen on M3 Max.

The improved thermal performance comes from both chip-level efficiencies and Apple's redesigned thermal architecture in the MacBook Pro chassis. The vapor chamber cooling system has been expanded, and the fans now spin at lower speeds under equivalent loads, resulting in quieter operation overall.

Surface temperatures tell a similar story. During a 30-minute Final Cut Pro export, the keyboard deck reached 102°F on the M5 Max versus 108°F on the M3 Max. The bottom panel was correspondingly cooler, making the machine more comfortable for lap use during intensive tasks.

Battery Life: Generational Improvements

Battery life was already exceptional on the M3 Max MacBook Pro, but the M5 Max pushes further. In our standardized web browsing test at 200 nits brightness, the M5 Max lasted 22 hours versus 18 hours for M3 Max. Video playback achieved 24 hours compared to 20 hours.

Our professional workflow test, which alternates between document editing, video playback, and compilation tasks, ran for 17 hours on M5 Max compared to 13.5 hours on M3 Max. These improvements reflect both the efficiency gains of the M5 architecture and optimizations in power state management.

When you do need to charge, the new 140W MagSafe charger fills the battery from 0 to 50% in 30 minutes, with a full charge taking approximately 90 minutes. The charger now includes a detachable USB-C cable compliant with USB Power Delivery 3.1, allowing it to charge other devices at full speed.

Display and Audio

The 16.2-inch Liquid Retina XDR display remains unchanged from the M3 Max generation, which is no criticism—it remains one of the best laptop displays available. The 3456 x 2234 resolution, 1000 nits sustained brightness, and ProMotion 120Hz refresh rate continue to deliver exceptional visual fidelity. The display supports the P3 wide color gamut and includes hardware calibration for professional color work.

HDR content looks stunning, with the display reaching 1600 nits peak brightness for HDR content. The contrast ratio of 1,000,000:1 ensures that blacks are truly black, making this an excellent choice for video editors and photographers who need accurate preview of their work.

The speaker system has been refined, with slightly improved bass response and more distinct stereo separation. The six-speaker array with force-canceling woofers remains class-leading, eliminating the need for external speakers during casual listening and video calls.

Port Selection and Connectivity

The port selection matches the M3 Max generation: three Thunderbolt 5 ports (USB-C) supporting charging, DisplayPort, and Thunderbolt 5; an HDMI 2.1 port; an SDXC card slot; and a MagSafe 3 charging port. The addition of Thunderbolt 5 doubles bandwidth compared to Thunderbolt 4, enabling faster connections to external storage and docks.

External display support has improved. The M5 Max can drive two 6K displays at 60Hz simultaneously, or a single 8K display. This makes the MacBook Pro M5 Max a capable workstation when connected to external monitors.

Wireless connectivity includes Wi-Fi 7 and Bluetooth 5.3. Wi-Fi 7 offers theoretical maximum speeds of 46 Gbps, though real-world improvements over Wi-Fi 6E depend heavily on router and network conditions.

Build Quality and Keyboard

The chassis design remains consistent with the 2021 redesign that introduced the notch, MagSafe, and expanded port selection. The Space Black color option continues to resist fingerprints better than the previous Space Gray, and the aluminum unibody construction provides the rigidity and premium feel expected at this price point.

The keyboard features Apple's latest generation of scissors switches, offering 1mm of travel with a satisfying tactile response. The Force Touch trackpad remains the industry's best, with precise cursor control and uniform haptic feedback across its entire surface.

Value Analysis: Who Should Upgrade?

The MacBook Pro M5 Max with 36GB unified memory starts at $3,499, with the 48GB configuration at $3,699 and 64GB at $3,999. The M3 Max MacBook Pro remains available at $2,999 for the base 36GB configuration, representing meaningful savings for those who don't need maximum performance.

M3 Max owners should strongly consider upgrading if they regularly encounter workloads that push against performance limits. Video editors working with 8K footage, 3D artists running real-time ray tracing, developers compiling large codebases, and machine learning practitioners will see substantial improvements that justify the investment.

M3 Max owners whose workloads don't regularly challenge their machine's capabilities will find the M5 Max's improvements interesting but not transformative. The battery life gains and thermal improvements are real, but not dramatic enough to warrant a new purchase cycle on their own.

For M1 Max or Intel-based MacBook Pro owners, the upgrade to M5 Max represents multiple generational leaps and will feel transformational across virtually every metric. The combined improvements in CPU, GPU, Neural Engine, battery life, and connectivity make this the most compelling MacBook Pro upgrade in several years.

The Bottom Line

The MacBook Pro M5 Max is without question the most powerful Mac laptop ever created. It demolishes the M3 Max in every benchmark while delivering better battery life and quieter operation. For professionals whose livelihoods depend on compute-intensive work, the M5 Max's capabilities translate directly to time saved and work completed.

The upgrade from M3 Max is not automatic—it depends heavily on whether your workflows actually require this level of performance. But for those who do, the M5 Max delivers in quantities that justify its premium pricing. Apple has once again raised the bar for what a professional laptop can accomplish, and the M5 Max stands as a testament to the continued evolution of Apple Silicon.
